About Me

Clear Lake is naturally eutrophic and nutrient flows from surrounding industries further promote algae growth. These contaminants, in addition to sewage, erosion, and agricultural runoff, exacerbate eutrophic conditions and negatively impact water quality. Under certain conditions, a type of algae called cyanobacteria “bloom” and cause unpleasant aesthetic conditions and hazardous health impacts. The bacteria in the lake produce cyanotoxins that are harmful to human health and deplete dissolved oxygen (DO) levels required to sustain aquatic life. The historical presence of cyanobacteria in Clear Lake must be addressed to maintain a thriving recreational industry and protect the community from algal toxins.

This project aims to develop an algal bloom prediction system for Clear Lake through the use of a real-time water quality sensor network and an integrated Water Quality Index (WQI). This WQI will be used to determine the relative risk of hazardous algal bloom occurences in the lake based on historical data. The ability to monitor and predict bloom conditions in advance allows stakeholders to minimize negative health and welfare impacts on surrounding communities.
