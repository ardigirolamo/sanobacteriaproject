About Me


I am a graduate student pursuing my Ph.D. in Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of California, Davis with Dr. Holly Oldroyd. From 2013-2017, I was an undergraduate student at the University of Nevada, Reno, where I worked with Dr. Heather Holmes in the ATAQ Lab. Typically as a graduate researcher, I am numerically modeling the atmosphere, preparing weather stations for deployment across California, learning new Python tricks, and developing new baked goods!


My research interests are tied to the interactions between the atmosphere and the earth’s surface. Specifically, I am interested in how the earth’s surface couples with the atmosphere, and influence energy partitioning, near surface meteorology, planetary boundary layer dynamics, water availability, and decision making processes that revolve around this area. My research experience to date has been focused on modeling land surface interactions within the Central Valley, California to try and understand the influence of irrigation on the land-surface-atmosphere nexus. I work in Python, Matlab, shell, and Fortran to develop new model code, analyze data, and visualize results with a combination of traditional coding methods and data science techniques (evolutionary algorithms, self-organizing maps, etc.).
